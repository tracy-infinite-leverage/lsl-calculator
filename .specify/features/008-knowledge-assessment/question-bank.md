# Payroll Knowledge Assessment — Question Bank

**Format:** 35 multiple-choice questions across 6 categories.
**Source:** Supplied by operator 2026-06-10.
**Currency:** FY2025–26 (SG rate 12% from 1 Jul 2025; WOI cap $180,000; STP finalisation 14 Jul).

> **Category mapping note.** The operator's hand-off labelled "End of Year — first 5 questions" then five other categories of six. The questions themselves are in a different order; the mapping below follows the **content** of each question. Six categories × (mostly) six questions = 35.

## Category 1 — Fair Work Compliance (Q1–Q6)

### Q1. Payslips must be given to an employee:
- A) When they request it
- **B) Within 1 working day of pay day** ✅
- C) Within 1 week of pay day
- D) Within 7 days of pay

### Q2. Which of the following entitlements are **not** covered by the National Employment Standards?
- A) Long Service Leave
- B) Personal/Carer's Leave
- **C) Workers Compensation** ✅
- D) Annual Leave

### Q3. Which one of the following statements is correct?
- A) Modern awards do not cover federal government or local government employees.
- B) Transitional arrangements mean that the rates of pay in most modern awards are introduced in four stages from July 2010 to July 2018.
- **C) The rate of pay for an employee under an enterprise agreement cannot be less than the relevant rate of pay under the modern award that would apply to the employee or under a national minimum wage order.** ✅
- D) An employee does not have to be attached to an award if they are paid the national minimum wage.

### Q4. Which of the following is **not** a National Employment Standard?
- A) Parental leave of 12 months (with a right to request an additional 12 months)
- B) Notice of Termination and Redundancy Pay
- **C) The maximum standard working week is 40 hours for full time employees, plus 'reasonable' additional hours** ✅
- D) The right for new employees to receive the Fair Work Information Statement

### Q5. For a large business employer (15 or more employees) the Casual Employment Information Statement (CEIS) must be given to all new casual employees:
- A) When they ask for it.
- **B) Upon commencement of employment, at 6 months, at 12 months and every 12 months thereafter** ✅
- C) On commencement of employment
- D) It is up to the employee to get a copy themselves from the Fair Work Ombudsman.

### Q6. Which of the following statements is **not** true?
- A) Employers have to keep written time and wages records for their employees for seven years
- B) Managers or higher income employees may not be covered by a modern award even if one applies to the industry in which they work
- C) Fair Work Inspectors use time and wages records to find out what an employee is entitled to and whether they're getting those entitlements
- **D) Employee time and wage records must be kept as a hard copy that is easily accessible to Fair Work Inspectors** ✅

## Category 2 — Superannuation (Q7–Q12)

### Q7. The superannuation guarantee rate effective 1 July 2025 is:
- A) 9.5%
- **B) 12%** ✅
- C) 10.5%
- D) 11.5%

### Q8. Which of the following payments are **not** 'Ordinary Time Earnings' according to SGR2009/2?
- A) Afternoon and Night Shift penalty rates
- B) Commission payments
- **C) Overtime** ✅
- D) Christmas bonus

### Q9. What is the maximum super contributions base?
- A) The minimum amount on which an employer must make superannuation guarantee contributions.
- B) The amount used to determine a defined benefit fund contribution.
- **C) It is used to determine the maximum limit on any individual employee's earnings base for each quarter of any financial year. Employers do not have to provide the minimum support for the part of earnings above this limit for SG Super.** ✅
- D) Is a fixed amount that is not indexed.

### Q10. Employers would generally offer Superannuation Choice of Fund to:
- A) Government employees in unfunded public sector super schemes.
- **B) Eligible employees who are commencing work with the employer, within 28 days of their start date.** ✅
- C) Employees who remain a member of a defined benefit fund that is in surplus or who have accrued their maximum benefit.
- D) Employees covered by a range of employment agreements that specify a super fund.

### Q11. If an employee does not provide their superannuation fund, you must:
- A) Pay their super contributions into the company's nominated default fund.
- B) Withhold super contributions until the employee provides their fund details.
- **C) Request their stapled super fund details from the ATO and pay into that fund if one exists; if not, pay into the company's nominated default fund.** ✅
- D) Pay their super contributions directly to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) as a holding account.

### Q12. Which of the following are Reportable Employer Superannuation Contributions (RESC)?
- A) Contributions in excess of 12%
- B) Matching employer contributions under an Enterprise Agreement
- **C) Contributions made by an employer that exceed mandated levels and which the employee can influence** ✅
- D) After tax (voluntary) superannuation contributions

## Category 3 — Payroll Tax (Q13–Q18)

### Q13. Payroll Tax is payable on:
- A) All salaries and wages paid to employees and contractors working in Australia only.
- **B) All salaries and wages paid to employees and relevant contractors that exceed the exemption threshold.** ✅
- C) All organisations including Public Benevolent Institutions, public or non-profit hospitals and charitable organisations.
- D) All salaries and wages paid but not including amounts forgone under an effective salary sacrifice arrangement.

### Q14. Payroll tax rates and thresholds:
- **A) Vary between States and Territories.** ✅
- B) Are indexed in line with Average Weekly Earnings.
- C) Increase proportionately with salaries and wages.
- D) Are set by the Federal Government.

### Q15. Payroll Tax harmonisation refers to:
- A) The exclusion of certain payments from the definition of 'salaries and wages'.
- **B) The aligning of legislative provisions across Australian States and Territories.** ✅
- C) The setting of uniform payroll tax rates and thresholds across all States and Territories.
- D) The restriction placed on information sharing between State and Revenue offices.

### Q16. Which of the following payments are **not** subject to Payroll Tax in any jurisdiction?
- A) Salary sacrifice superannuation contributions.
- B) Long service leave payments.
- **C) Reimbursement of expenses.** ✅
- D) Redundancy payments in excess of the tax free portion.

### Q17. Which of the following statements is true?
- A) Wages for employees working overseas are not subject to payroll tax.
- B) Leave payments on termination are not subject to payroll tax.
- C) Back payments to employees relating to prior financial years are not subject to payroll tax.
- **D) Employers hiring employees through an employment agency are not subject to payroll tax on payments made to the agency.** ✅

### Q18. An employer required to lodge Payroll Tax returns should lodge them:
- A) Once a year.
- **B) Monthly and annually.** ✅
- C) Not on the months that are nil.
- D) Quarterly.

## Category 4 — Leave (Q19–Q24)

### Q19. Which of the following statements is true?
- A) Under the NES, all employees are entitled to annual leave, personal/carer's leave, long service leave and public holiday leave.
- B) Casual employees are not entitled to long service leave.
- **C) Casual employees are entitled to parental leave.** ✅
- D) An employee cannot use all their personal/sick leave as carer's leave.

### Q20. An employee does **not** accrue annual leave whilst on paid:
- A) Long service leave
- **B) Government funded parental leave** ✅
- C) Personal leave
- D) Annual leave

- **A) Are subject to PAYG.** ✅
- B) Accrue annual leave and personal leave entitlements.
- C) Are subject to Payroll Tax.
- D) Are subject to SG (i.e. the employer must make superannuation contributions on these payments).

- A) Tasmania and South Australia
- **B) Northern Territory and South Australia** ✅
- C) Tasmania and ACT
- D) NSW and Northern Territory

### Q23. An employee may cash out annual leave only if:
- **A) An award or agreement permits the practice or the employee is award free.** ✅
- B) The employee has completed five years service.
- C) The employee has an entitlement in excess of eight weeks.
- D) When both parties agree to it at any amount.

### Q24. Which of the following statements regarding Public Holidays is correct?

- B) Employees cannot refuse to work on a Public Holiday.
- C) Public Holidays cannot be substituted for other days.
- **D) Employees aren't entitled to be paid for public holidays if they are on unpaid leave on both sides of a public holiday.** ✅

## Category 5 — Terminations (Q25–Q30)

### Q25. Which of the following payments are **not** Employment Termination Payments?
- A) Payment in lieu of notice
- B) Ex-gratia payment

- D) Unused personal leave and RDO payments on termination

### Q26. The tax free portion of a genuine redundancy payment:
- A) Appears as the "ETP tax free component" on an income statement.
- **B) Is indexed each year.** ✅

### Q27. An employee's Whole of Income Cap for 2025–26 is calculated using which figure?
- A) $245,000
- **B) $180,000** ✅

- D) $235,000

### Q28. Which of the following statements is true on terminations?

- B) Super is not paid on any payments of Notice.
- C) Employees must be paid notice if they are terminated because of serious misconduct.
- D) All employees employed for a specific period are also entitled to Notice on termination.

### Q29. Lump Sum A on an Income Statement does **not** reflect:
- A) Unused long service leave that accrued after 15 August 1978 but before 18 August 1993.
- B) Unused holiday pay and other leave related payments that accrued before 18 August 1993.
- C) Unused long service leave accrued after 17 August 1993 or unused holiday pay and other leave related payments, where the amount was paid in connection with a payment that includes (or consists of) a genuine redundancy payment, an early retirement scheme payment or the invalidity segment of an employment termination payment or superannuation benefit.
- **D) Unused leave accrued after 17 August 1993 on resignation.** ✅

### Q30. A notice period by the employer would be required in which of the following situations:
- A) Termination of employment for a casual employee.
- B) Dismissal for serious misconduct, fraud or theft.
- C) Resignation by employee.

## Currency-sensitive items (need annual review)

These questions reference FY-dated thresholds and rates. Re-validate at every new FY rollover before reusing the bank.

- **Q7** — SG rate effective 1 Jul 2025: 12%. Next legislated change: TBA.
- **Q22** — LSL accrual rate exception (NT + SA). Stable but verify against current Acts.
- **Q26** — Genuine-redundancy tax-free base/per-year-of-service amounts are indexed annually.
- **Q27** — Whole of Income Cap 2025–26: $180,000. Re-check each FY.
- **Q31** — STP finalisation due date 14 July (currently stable for >20 employees; 31 July for closely-held payees — out of scope for this question).
